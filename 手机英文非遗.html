Chongqing intangible cultural heritage

The iron-striking flower is an intangible cultural heritage of Chongqing, which originated from the iron-making technique of the Ming Dynasty. During the performance, the molten iron is struck into the air and sparks bloom, symbolizing the exorcism of evil spirits and blessings, which requires superb skills and has been passed down to this day.

Chongqing Dragon Dance (Tongliang Dragon Dance) is a national intangible cultural heritage, which originated in the Tang and Song dynasties and was popular in the Ming and Qing dynasties. With the dragon as a prop, the dance posture is majestic, symbolizing auspiciousness and festivity, which is an important folk culture in Chongqing.

Chongqing suona is a traditional wind instrument of Bayu, with a high-pitched and loud timbre, often used in weddings, funerals, festivals, inheritance for a hundred years, rich in local characteristics, and is an important representative of Chongqing folk art.

Chongqing Lantern Opera, also known as Liangshan Lantern Opera, is a traditional drama in Liangping District, Chongqing, which originated in the Zhengde period of the Ming Dynasty, and is characterized by "laughing" and "twisting and jumping", and is often performed in folk activities such as the Spring Festival and the Lantern Festival.
